Early Life

E.T.A. Hoffman was a promising poet, a jurist, and musician born during the German Romantic Era. His childhood was instable, as his parents were divorced, with one being an alcoholic, and the other being often described as a hysterical and obsessively orderly woman, influencing the way he was raised and shaping his writing style. Starting from his early teenage years, Hoffman began painting and creating art compositions, and when he worked in the Prussian provinces, many said he began to become dissolute.

The Effect of his Life on his Work

Many of his tales feature main characters who are demented, either permanently or temporarily, under the impact of events that their minds cannot handle. His stories often showcase the way everyday life and fantasy coexisted and often merged, becoming one. Throughout his life, he suffered from the misery and constraints of Prussian reality, which he portrayed in his novels. He believed that only artists and children could escape the constraints of everyday life thanks to their imagination and befriended them at every opportunity, spontaneously come up with stories and fairy tales for them that were never written down. Eventually, he was exiled and became isolated from society, where he began drawing, composing, and writing in his diary.
