The Sandman - Themes Table (Shivali)

Category Detail Connection to The Sandman
Historical Context German Romanticism (1800–1830) Emphasizes emotion, fear, and the supernatural.
Theme Technology vs. Human Emotion Olympia represents artificial perfection replacing real relationships.
Character Impact Nathaniel’s obsession and paranoia Shows how trauma and imagination drive his downfall.
Symbolism The Eyes Symbolize identity, vulnerability, and control.


Characters (Alyssa)

Character Relationship to Nathaniel Description
Nathaniel Self A young man and the protagonist of the story. Nathaniel has a lifelong fascination with tales of the unusual, the imaginary, and the grotesque, a tendency he has had since he was a young boy. This spurned from his nightmares of Coppelius, whom he believes is the Sandman, a fictional villain who throws and into the eyes of children until they fall out. He is prone to fears and fantasies taking over his thoughts until he can no longer tell what is real. His interest and obsession with coppelius soon yields to darker, more cynical thought and eventually, insanity.
Clara Fianceé of Nathaniel; Lothario's sister She is a sweet, sensible young woman who tries to counteract some of Nathaniel’s darker fears, offering reasonable explanations for them. She tries to appeal to Nathaniel’s better nature and despairs when he shows the dark side of his personality. Clara represents the conflict between the Enlightenment era's rational thinking, combined with the Romantic value of emotion and imagination.
Lothario Nathaniel's best friend; Clara's brother Fiercly protective of his sister, also a companion to Nathaniel.
Coppelius/Coppola Coppelius: Family acquaintance; source of Nathaniel's nightmares and paranoia
Coppola: A merchant and vendor at Nathaniel's university		 Unbeknowst to Nathaniel for most of the story, Coppelius and Coppola are the one and same. Monstrous in appearance, with exaggerated features, but even more monstrous in the way he frightens or disgusts the protaganists. Coppelius shows up at key moments in the story, taunting and terrifying Nathaniel, and making many cryptic references to eyes. Nathaniel is terrified of him, and finds Coppelius responsible for the death of his father.
Olympia Love interest A beautiful young woman who lives across from Nathaniel. Nathaniel becomes infatuated with her after looking at her though a telescope he bought from Coppola/Coppelius. She is a machine, but Nathaniel is blind to this fact and believes her to be a real person.
Spalanzani Nathaniel's professor; Olympia's 'dad' A professor of physics at the university Nathaniel attends. Known as the father of Olympia.
The Father Father Works with Coppelius on projects. Ends up being killed when working on one of these such experiments in what is believed to be an accident.
The Mother Mother Does not like Coppelius much, likely due to him being the reason for her father's constant distance. However, she reluctantly puts up with it.
