Our Book:

Our book is the Sandman by E.T. Hoffman

"The Sandman" is a psychological horror story that explores themes of fear, reality versus fantasy, and the impact of childhood trauma through the experiences of Nathanael, who becomes obsessed with the figure of the Sandman.

Historical Analysis

  • Hoffmann lived during the height of German Romanticism, and his personal clashes with Prussian authority shaped the dark, rebellious, and anti-rational tone of The Sandman.
  • Rapid technological change created fears of dehumanization, and Romantic thinkers worried machines would replace human emotion, individuality, and relationships—reflected in Hoffmann’s portrayal of automatons as unsettling and threatening.
  • Psychology and psychoanalysis drew heavily from Hoffmann, with Freud using The Sandman to explain “the uncanny” and later psychiatrists interpreting his characters as early depictions of schizophrenia or “splitting insanity.”
